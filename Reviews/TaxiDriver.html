My thoughts on the movie Taxi Driver (1976)

Wow. This movie is generally regarded very highly, and quite rightly so. Atmospheric, brilliantly acted, unsettling, and open to as many interpretations as you wish to throw at it. The ending blew my mind, and a quick internet search revealed there has been much discussion of those final ten minutes over the forty-plus years since this movie came out. To me (but clearly not to the majority of viewers) it was obvious that the events after the almighty shoot-out were played purely from Travis Bickle's imagination - otherwise, how would he have got off so lightly with three cold-blooded murders? I think that those final scenes showed his motivations and hopes before the bloodbath, rather than (as some have suggested) his dying thoughts. But the more I thought about that ending, the more interesting the whole movie became. Given the fact that Travis is a pretty creepy guy, isn't it a bit odd that women and girls so thoughtlessly agreed to go out for a coffee with him? Could it be that everything in the movie was played from his imagination? Or maybe not? This, ultimately, is the power of the movie; so craftily written and directed that many interpretations are possible. The only irksome thing about the movie is the frequent product-placement shots for a certain soft beverage. Otherwise, a masterpiece.
